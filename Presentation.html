The Problem

Why Traditional Approaches Fail

Policymakers lack granular, evidence-based tools to prioritize rural infrastructure investments.

Population Myth

Relying on raw population data misleads — sheer size does not equal growth potential. Large but isolated villages get over-prioritized.

No Spatial Context

Without connectivity and proximity metrics, decisions ignore the network effects that drive real economic spillover from urban hubs.

Untapped Satellite Data

Nighttime luminosity (VIIRS) provides a powerful proxy for economic activity, yet remains underutilized in rural planning.

The Solution

Identifying Growth Engines, Not Just Villages

The RGPI filters thousands of locations to isolate the top 100 villages best positioned to serve as regional economic catalysts.

  • Integrates spatial, demographic, and satellite data into a unified index
  • Applies weighted gravity-based scoring aligned with economic theory
  • Produces an interactive map + ranked list for direct policy action
  • Scalable framework applicable across states and regions

The Algorithm

Weighted Scoring Formula

A weighted linear combination aligned with economic gravity theory.

Score = (Connectivity × 0.5) + (Density × 0.3) + (Urban Impact × 0.2)

Results

Power Law Distribution

Growth potential is not evenly spread — it concentrates in a few strategic nodes.

  • Top 100 villages represent the extreme right tail of this distribution

Engineering

Technical Challenges Solved

How we ensured model reliability and statistical integrity.

Data Skewness

High-value urban clusters washed out subtle rural variations. Solution: Log-transformation (np.log1p) compressed the range while preserving relative ordering.

Model Validation

If connectivity correlation had been positive, the inverse formula would be flawed. Confirmed: -0.21 validates the methodology.

Data Completeness

Left joins + zero imputation ensured no village was dropped due to missing auxiliary metadata, preserving spatial coverage.

Summary

Key Takeaways

01

Location Over Size

Urban proximity (0.62 correlation) dwarfs population (0.01) as a growth predictor. Policy should prioritize peri-urban villages, not just populous ones.

02

Power Law Confirms Targeting

Growth potential follows a long-tail distribution — blanket rural policies are inefficient. Focused investment in top nodes yields maximum ROI.

03

NCR Dominance

48 of the top 100 villages cluster around Delhi NCR — India's largest urban agglomeration creates an unmatched spillover corridor spanning UP and Haryana.

04

Scalable Framework

The RGPI methodology is repeatable across states, adaptable to new data sources, and can integrate additional variables like road networks or electrification data.
